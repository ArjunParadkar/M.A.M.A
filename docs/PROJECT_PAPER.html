Market & National Need

The United States faces a critical manufacturing deficit that represents both a national challenge and an unprecedented opportunity for distributed domestic production through additive manufacturing and digital fabrication.

Key Observations

  • Distributed manufacturing is scaling rapidly; the 3D printing and digital fabrication market is projected to reach $83.9 billion by 2030, growing at 25.8% annually. This growth represents a massive opportunity to harness distributed production capacity.
  • The problem isn't "no capacity" — it's coordination, access, and trust for distributed manufacturing networks. With 2,400+ Fab Labs and makerspaces across the United States, plus hundreds of thousands of individuals with 3D printers, CNC machines, and laser cutters, the production capacity exists but remains fragmented and underutilized.
  • Trade deficits create urgency; the $918.4 billion U.S. trade deficit represents a massive opportunity to shift production back to domestic sources. Even a 1% shift from imported manufactured goods to distributed domestic production represents over $9 billion in economic activity.
  • On-demand manufacturing is the future; as additive manufacturing costs decrease and quality improves, distributed production becomes economically viable for small-batch and custom manufacturing at scale.

Users & Use Cases

Project MAMA operates as a two-sided marketplace connecting clients seeking manufacturing services with distributed manufacturers across the United States.

A) Client Users

Individual Inventors — Seeking prototype production or small-batch manufacturing for product development
Small Businesses — Requiring manufacturing capacity without capital investment in equipment or long-term contracts
Corporations / Procurement Teams — Diversifying supply chains, reducing lead times through parallel manufacturing, and accessing distributed production networks

B) Manufacturer Users

Individuals with Equipment — Makers with 3D printers, CNC machines, or other manufacturing equipment seeking to monetize idle capacity
Small Shops — Local manufacturing facilities with specialized equipment seeking access to broader client base
Contract Manufacturers — Established shops looking to optimize utilization and access AI-powered matching and scheduling

Use Cases: Job Types & Fulfillment Patterns

Open Request
Large quantity orders (200+ units) split among multiple manufacturers. System automatically distributes units based on capacity, quality, and rank scores. Example: 2000-unit order distributed across 15-20 manufacturers.
Quick Service
Speed-optimized orders with auto-assignment to top-ranked manufacturers. Trusted by clients for rapid turnaround. System matches and assigns within minutes of posting.
Closed Request
Single manufacturer bid/accept workflow. Client reviews top matches, selects preferred manufacturer. Traditional one-to-one manufacturing relationship.
Closed Commission
Recurring weekly production quotas. Long-term relationships with established manufacturers for consistent product supply. Example: 8 units per week over 12-month period.

Value Proposition

Client Benefits

Faster Lead Times via Parallel Manufacturing

Large orders split across multiple manufacturers complete in parallel, reducing delivery time by 50-70% compared to single-facility production. Community manufacturing projects (e.g., 2000-unit orders) deliver in days rather than weeks.

Supply Chain Resilience

Distributed production nodes eliminate single points of failure. Unlike traditional manufacturing reliant on one factory, distributed networks continue operating even if individual nodes experience delays or disruptions.

Tariff & Shipping Risk Mitigation

Domestic production eliminates international shipping delays, tariff uncertainty, and geopolitical supply chain risks. All manufacturing occurs within the United States, reducing lead time variance and customs complications.

Transparent AI-Powered Pricing

Fair pay estimator (F2 model) provides transparent, data-driven pricing based on material costs, labor hours, complexity, and market rates. Clients receive upfront cost estimates before committing to orders.

Manufacturer Benefits

Monetize Idle Capacity

Turn underutilized equipment into revenue streams. Manufacturers can accept jobs that match their available capacity, optimizing equipment utilization without requiring full-time commitments.

Access to Contracts Otherwise Unreachable

Small shops and individual operators gain access to contracts typically reserved for large manufacturers. AI-powered matching connects manufacturers with compatible jobs regardless of company size.

Reputation System & Quality Tooling

Build trust through verified quality scores, completion rates, and client ratings. AI-powered quality check (F3) provides objective quality assessment, enabling manufacturers to demonstrate capability and build reputation.

AI-Optimized Scheduling

Workflow scheduling (F4) optimizes task allocation across available devices, balancing deadlines, profit margins, and capacity constraints. Manufacturers maximize earnings while meeting delivery commitments.

Proven Precedent

Distributed production has proven effective under crisis constraints. During the COVID-19 pandemic, maker and manufacturing networks produced personal protective equipment (PPE) at massive scale, demonstrating that coordinated distributed manufacturing can meet urgent national needs. Manufacturing collectives produced millions of face shields, masks, and ventilator components using 3D printing, CNC machining, and laser cutting across hundreds of distributed facilities.

Project MAMA's claim: We make distributed production feasible as an everyday system through AI-powered pricing (fair pay estimation), intelligent matching (manufacturer ranking), automated quality control (vision-based QC), and optimized scheduling (workflow algorithms). Where crisis-driven networks operated through ad-hoc coordination, MAMA provides the infrastructure, trust mechanisms, and economic incentives for sustainable distributed manufacturing at scale.

Product Workflow & Feature Set

Project MAMA delivers an end-to-end platform connecting clients and manufacturers through AI-powered matching, quality assurance, and workflow optimization.

1
Client Uploads STL + Specs

Client uploads 3D design file (STL format) with detailed specifications: material (e.g., 6061-T6 Aluminum), tolerance (±0.005" in thou), quantity, deadline, finish details (polished, brushed, sandblasted), coatings (anodized, powder coat, paint with color specification), and manufacturing type requirements (CNC, 3D printing, injection molding, etc.).

2
AI Analysis Screen

System analyzes STL geometry and specifications, then calculates price/time estimates and identifies top maker matches. F2 Pay Estimator provides suggested pay with range ($807-$1,092 for example order). F1 Maker Ranking identifies top 10-20 manufacturers ranked by equipment match, capacity, quality scores, and historical performance. Estimated completion time displayed for each match.

3
Posting Mode Selection

Client chooses posting mode: Open Request (multi-manufacturer distribution), Quick Service (auto-assignment), Closed Request (single manufacturer selection), or Closed Commission (recurring quota). For open requests with quantity ≥100 units, system automatically triggers distribution algorithm.

4
Maker Discovery & Acceptance

Manufacturers view job recommendations in dashboard, filtered by compatibility (material, tolerance tier, equipment type). For open requests, manufacturers can accept partial quantities (e.g., 150 of 2000 units) based on available capacity. System validates acceptance against remaining unassigned quantity and manufacturer capacity constraints.

5
Production Tracking

Workflow page displays all active jobs with assignments, showing which manufacturer is producing what quantity, estimated delivery dates, and progress (completed/assigned). For multi-manufacturer jobs, client views aggregated progress across all assignments. F4 Workflow Scheduling optimizes manufacturer task sequencing across available devices.

6
QC Submission

Manufacturer uploads 4-6 photos of completed part from multiple angles. F3 Vision Quality Check model compares photos to original STL file, analyzing dimensional accuracy, surface quality, similarity, and anomaly detection. Returns QC score (0-1), status (pass/review/fail), and detailed notes (e.g., "Dimensions within tolerance", "Minor surface imperfections detected").

7
Shipping & Payment

Manufacturer enters carrier (UPS, FedEx, USPS, DHL) and tracking number. System updates job status to "accepted" and triggers payment transaction (status: "pending" → "authorized" → "paid"). Client receives shipping notifications and tracking updates. Payment processed upon delivery confirmation.

8
Messaging & Ratings

In-job messaging enables client-manufacturer communication throughout production cycle. Upon completion, client rates manufacturer on quality, timeliness, and communication. Ratings aggregated into manufacturer trust score, influencing future ranking (F1) and match probability.

Key Differentiator: Auto-Distribution Algorithm

For large open requests (200+ units), system automatically distributes quantity across multiple manufacturers using weighted algorithm: Rank Score × Capacity Score × Quality Score. Ensures proportional allocation based on capability while maintaining minimum unit thresholds (10+ units per manufacturer) and capacity constraints. Example: 2000-unit order distributed across 15-20 manufacturers, with each receiving 80-200 units based on their combined scores. No manual coordination required — system handles matching, assignment, and workload balancing automatically.

AI Systems — All 8 Features

Project MAMA employs four core AI models plus four supporting algorithms to enable intelligent matching, pricing, quality assurance, workflow optimization, time estimation, earnings tracking, recommendations, and rating aggregation.

F1 — Maker Ranking
Inputs:
  • Job specs (material, tolerance_tier, quantity, deadline_days)
  • Maker equipment inventory (device types, capabilities)
  • Maker materials array (compatible materials)
  • Historical performance (average_rating, total_jobs_completed, quality_score)
  • Capacity & quality scores (0-1 normalized)
  • Location data (distance calculation)
Outputs:
  • Ranked list of manufacturers (rank_score: 0-1)
  • Explanations JSON (why each manufacturer ranked highly)
  • Completion time estimate (hours, delivery date)
  • Match confidence scores
Evaluation Metrics:
  • Match success rate (job acceptance by top-ranked manufacturers)
  • On-time delivery rate (completion within estimated deadline)
  • Client satisfaction (post-completion ratings for matched manufacturers)
F2 — Fair Pay Estimator
Inputs:
  • Material type & cost per unit
  • Time estimate (setup_hours, estimated_hours per unit)
  • Complexity score (tolerance tier, finish requirements, coatings)
  • Urgency multiplier (deadline vs. standard_delivery_days)
  • Market rate per hour (regional adjustments)
Outputs:
  • Suggested pay (USD, fair market price)
  • Range (low: suggested × 0.85, high: suggested × 1.15)
  • Breakdown JSON (material_cost, labor_cost, overhead, margin)
  • Model version (for audit trail)
Evaluation Metrics:
  • Dispute rate (jobs with pricing disputes / total jobs)
  • Acceptance rate (manufacturer acceptance of suggested pay)
  • Variance (actual vs. estimated cost post-completion)
F3 — Vision Quality Check
Inputs:
  • STL file (3D model geometry, volume, surface area, bounding box)
  • Evidence photos (4-6 images from multiple angles)
  • Tolerance tier (low/medium/high, affects pass threshold)
  • Critical dimensions (optional: width, height, depth expectations)
Outputs:
  • QC score (0-1, overall quality assessment)
  • Status (pass / review / fail, based on threshold)
  • Similarity score (0-1, visual similarity to STL)
  • Dimensional accuracy (0-1, dimension compliance)
  • Surface quality (0-1, finish assessment)
  • Anomaly score (0-1, defect likelihood, higher = fewer defects)
  • Notes array (issues found)
Evaluation Metrics:
  • Agreement with human review (F1 score: precision/recall vs. expert QC assessment)
  • False pass rate (QC approved but later rejected by client)
  • False fail rate (QC rejected but later accepted by client)
F4 — Workflow Scheduling
Inputs:
  • Active jobs array (job_id, deadline, estimated_hours, device_type requirement, priority)
  • Available devices array (device_id, device_type, status, capabilities JSON)
  • Current time & business hours
  • Manufacturer capacity constraints
Outputs:
  • Optimized schedule (tasks ordered by priority with start/end times)
  • Device assignments (which device handles which job, when)
  • Utilization metrics (device_utilization %, idle_time)
  • Timeline visualization data
  • Optimization score (0-1, how well schedule meets constraints)
Evaluation Metrics:
  • On-time delivery rate (jobs completed by deadline)
  • Utilization improvement (before vs. after AI scheduling, % increase)
  • Profit optimization (earnings per hour improvement)
Time Calculator
Inputs:
  • Manufacturer historical completion data (past job durations, device types)
  • Job specifications (quantity, complexity, tolerance tier)
  • Device capabilities (printer speed, CNC machining time, setup time)
  • Current workload (active jobs, device availability)
Outputs:
  • Estimated completion time (hours, days)
  • Delivery date prediction (accounting for current workload)
  • Confidence interval (time range based on historical variance)
Purpose:
  • Learns from manufacturer past behavior to predict accurate completion times
  • Improves over time as more jobs are completed
  • Used by F1 Maker Ranking for deadline matching
Earnings Calculator
Inputs:
  • Completed job records (pay received, job_id, completion_date)
  • Active job assignments (pending pay, estimated completion)
  • Time period (daily, weekly, monthly, all-time)
Outputs:
  • Total earnings (USD, all-time or period-specific)
  • Earnings by job (breakdown of revenue per completed job)
  • Earnings trends (growth over time, revenue per week/month)
  • Projected earnings (based on active jobs)
Purpose:
  • Track manufacturer revenue and financial performance
  • Display earnings metrics in manufacturer dashboard
  • Enable financial planning and capacity optimization
Project Recommender
Inputs:
  • Manufacturer profile (equipment, materials, capacity, quality_score)
  • Available jobs (open requests, quick services, closed requests)
  • Pay-to-time ratio (high-value opportunities)
  • Manufacturer preferences (job types, material preferences)
Outputs:
  • Recommended jobs list (ranked by match score)
  • Match explanations (why each job is recommended)
  • Priority flags ("quick money", "high-value", "good fit")
Purpose:
  • Surfaces "quick money" jobs for manufacturers (high pay-to-time ratio)
  • Matches jobs to manufacturer equipment and capabilities
  • Helps manufacturers optimize earnings by accepting best-fit jobs
Rating Aggregator
Inputs:
  • Client ratings (1-5 stars, quality, timeliness, communication)
  • QC scores (F3 Vision Quality Check results)
  • On-time delivery rate (completed jobs vs. deadline)
  • Recency weighting (recent ratings weighted higher)
Outputs:
  • Average rating (weighted average of all ratings)
  • Rating distribution (5-star, 4-star, etc. breakdown)
  • Quality score (0-1, combined QC + client ratings)
  • Trust score (overall manufacturer reputation metric)
Purpose:
  • Prevents new users from dominating rankings (weighted by job count)
  • Combines multiple quality signals (ratings + QC + timeliness)
  • Updates manufacturer quality_score for F1 Maker Ranking

Additional Algorithm: Auto-Distribution

Auto-Distribution Algorithm

Weighted distribution: (rank_score × capacity_score × quality_score) / total_score. Ensures proportional allocation while respecting minimum unit thresholds and capacity constraints. Handles dynamic assignment as manufacturers accept/reject portions. Automatically distributes large open requests (200+ units) across multiple manufacturers based on combined scores, eliminating manual coordination.

System Architecture Overview

Include: Frontend → API Routes → FastAPI Backend → AI Models → Supabase Database/Storage

System Components
